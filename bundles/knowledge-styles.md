# Sage Minimal Flat Lay

## Visual intent

A calm, restrained fashion-editorial flat lay inspired by pale botanical neutrals. The product remains the only visual subject, with generous quiet space and low visual noise.

## Background

Use a pale, desaturated sage-green matte surface with subtle paper-like grain. Keep the plane continuous, clean, and free of visible seams or decorative objects. Variation should be gentle enough to support white and neutral products without looking digitally flat.

## Lighting

Use broad soft light from the upper left, low-to-medium contrast, controlled highlights, and a short diffused contact shadow falling subtly toward the lower right. Preserve natural grounding without heavy vignette or dramatic relighting.

## Preserve

Product Lock remains absolute: this style cannot alter the product's geometry, arrangement, color, material, construction, texture, reflections, or identity details. Background grading must stop at the product boundary and must not tint light-colored fabric or metal.

## Avoid

Avoid saturated mint, visible leaves, hard shadows, green color spill, fake paper edges, and copied reference objects. # Dark Luxury Jewelry

## Visual intent

A precise, high-end jewelry presentation with controlled drama, deep tonal separation, and realistic material response. Luxury comes from light control and finish, not added decoration.

## Background

Use a charcoal-to-black controlled matte or subtly satin surface. Maintain enough local separation to keep every locked edge readable. Do not replace the scene with a featureless black void.

## Lighting

Use deliberate soft key light plus restrained edge or strip highlights appropriate to the source geometry. Preserve physically plausible specular highlights and reflections, stone transparency, glass depth, and metal color. Keep the contact shadow controlled and believable.

## Preserve

Product Lock remains absolute: this style cannot alter the product's geometry, count, arrangement, metal color, finish, engravings, stones, transparency, component details, highlights, or identity. Rebuild only background reflections that must physically respond to the new environment.

## Avoid

Exclude all reference text, logos, brand marks, watermarks, captions, labels, typographic decoration, products, and props. Avoid crushed edges, blackened silver, yellowed white metal, invented gemstones, excessive sparkle, smoke, velvet boxes, and copied display stands. Output remains text-free unless the user supplies exact text and explicitly requests it.
