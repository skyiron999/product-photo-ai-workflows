# Gemini Limitations

The workflow constrains instructions and review; it cannot guarantee that a generative edit preserves every product pixel.

- Image-generation and editing availability can vary by supported language, country, age, Google Account type, work or school policy, subscription, app surface, and current usage quota.
- A style reference is not a strict mask. Gemini may reinterpret source details or mix information across uploaded images, so Product Lock requires human comparison.
- Fine typography, stitching, weave, pattern repeats, jewelry findings, stones, chain links, transparent materials, metal color, and reflections require close review.
- Ordinary photos do not provide calibrated exact color. Do not claim exact Hex, RGB, or production color accuracy without calibrated input.
- Multiple uploads increase role ambiguity. Plain-versus-decorative background alone is not reliable evidence of which image is the source.
- One targeted repair may be made from the original source. If it fails, restart with Safe Run from the original source; never feed a generated result into the next repair.
- Persistent fidelity failures are `MANUAL REVIEW` and may require conventional retouching or a reshoot.

Review Google's [current Gemini image requirements and availability](https://support.google.com/gemini/answer/14286560?hl=en) before documenting guarantees for a team.
