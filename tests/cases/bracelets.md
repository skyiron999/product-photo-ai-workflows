# Manual Case — Bracelets

## Objective

Verify continuous geometry, component spacing, and clasp or charm fidelity while replacing a tabletop background.

## Minimum source-image characteristics

Use a real photographed chain, bangle, cuff, beaded, or charm bracelet with a non-perfect resting shape, visible clasp, repeated components, polished areas, and at least one partial occlusion.

## Critical invariants

Item count, circumference or opening, band width, chain path, visible links, overlaps, clasp, charms, beads, stones, spacing, metal finish, color, highlights, and reflections.

## Reference-style requirements

The style reference should make lighting and surface intent clear while containing at least one unrelated object or text element to exclude.

## Safe Run checks

Confirm exact visible component locks, identify occluded sections as uncertain, and require `CONTINUE` before editing.

## Fast Run checks

Pause on ambiguous link boundaries or clasps. `NEXT PRODUCT` must not preserve component counts from the previous bracelet.

## Ecommerce checks

Check continuous clean edges, source geometry, background-first canvas expansion, safe padding, realistic grounding, and no product shrink or crop.

## Social checks

Check stronger mood and reflection integration without regularizing the bracelet into a perfect ellipse or moving charms.

## Failure conditions

Changed circumference, merged or added links, moved charm, replaced clasp, altered metal or stone color, impossible reflection, contamination, or unsupported fidelity claim.

## Asset-rights requirement

Add no image until creator, ownership or permission, source record, and redistribution license are complete.
