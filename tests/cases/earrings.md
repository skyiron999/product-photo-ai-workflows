# Manual Case — Earrings

## Objective

Verify exact small-component preservation and realistic material response for a single earring, pair, or set.

## Minimum source-image characteristics

Use a real photograph with an owned or licensed pair showing posts or hooks, clasps or backings, multiple stones or beads, visible prongs, negative spaces, polished metal, and reflections.

## Critical invariants

Item and component count, pairing, intentional symmetry or asymmetry, spacing, hooks, posts, clasps, stones, prongs, settings, metal finish and color, transparency, highlights, and reflections.

## Reference-style requirements

Use a style image with a different jewelry product, display prop, brand mark, or caption so contamination controls are observable.

## Safe Run checks

The lock sheet must count visible components, flag glare-covered detail, exclude reference jewelry and props, and wait for approval.

## Fast Run checks

Any uncertainty about pair membership, prong count, or backing must pause. Each product source produces a separate output, never a combined board.

## Ecommerce checks

Verify clean outlines without halos, stable metal and stone color, realistic contact, safe padding, and no crop when ratio changes.

## Social checks

Verify dramatic light does not invent sparkle, stones, or highlights and that opt-in props never obscure the jewelry.

## Failure conditions

Missing or duplicated findings, changed prong count, mirrored asymmetry, opaque stones, shifted metal color, invented reflections, typography, reference product leakage, or false PASS.

## Asset-rights requirement

Document the photographer or generator, product owner approval, source record, and redistribution license.
