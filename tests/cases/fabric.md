# Manual Case — Fabric

## Objective

Verify that textile material and repeated structure survive a background change.

## Minimum source-image characteristics

Use a real photographed swatch or folded textile with a visible cut edge, weave or knit, multiple folds, color variation, and either translucency, pile, or a repeated print.

## Critical invariants

Piece count, cut and selvage edges, fraying, weave or knit, pile direction, translucency, thickness, drape, fold placement, pattern scale and repeat, and observable color.

## Reference-style requirements

Use a reference with a distinct surface texture and light direction plus contaminating text, props, or unrelated products that must be excluded.

## Safe Run checks

The lock sheet must distinguish source texture from moire, identify uncertain fibers, and wait for `CONTINUE`.

## Fast Run checks

Unclear material face/reverse, merged layers, or unreadable pattern landmarks must pause instead of being invented.

## Ecommerce checks

Verify clean but authentic cut edges, quiet grounding, safe padding, source-ratio preservation, and background-first expansion for ratio changes.

## Social checks

Verify stronger mood does not warm, cool, smooth, thicken, or make the textile opaque; props remain opt-in.

## Failure conditions

Warped pattern repeat, cleaned frays, changed fold geometry, invented weave, lost translucency, texture smoothing, color drift, crop, or reference contamination.

## Asset-rights requirement

Record ownership or compatible license and a durable source or generation record before redistribution.
