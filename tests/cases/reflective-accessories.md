# Manual Case — Reflective Accessories

## Objective

Verify geometry and physically coherent environmental reflection changes on polished metal, glass, crystal, mirrored, or lacquered accessories.

## Minimum source-image characteristics

Use a real owned or licensed photograph with hard contours, bevels or engravings, both bright and dark reflections, transparent or translucent material, and a readable contact plane.

## Critical invariants

Geometry, openings, bevels, engravings, polished edges, metal color, glass or stone transparency, refraction cues, component count, base material, and highlight paths.

## Reference-style requirements

Use a reference whose light sources and surface can plausibly be reconstructed, plus visible products, props, or brand text that must not transfer.

## Safe Run checks

Separate intrinsic material color from reflected environment, flag glare-covered details, document excluded reference elements, and wait for approval.

## Fast Run checks

Pause if the requested environment cannot produce plausible highlights or if engravings and transparent boundaries are unreadable.

## Ecommerce checks

Verify readable edges on a restrained plane, controlled highlights, correct padding and canvas handling, no crop, and no false transparent cutout halo.

## Social checks

Verify dramatic background reflections remain physically plausible, do not hide engravings, and do not repaint metal or glass.

## Failure conditions

Bent geometry, erased engraving, opaque glass, shifted metal color, impossible reflections, added components, copied reference object or text, or unverified PASS.

## Asset-rights requirement

Record creator, product owner authorization where needed, source or generation record, and redistribution license before repository inclusion.
